Utopia, then what?

I find arguments surrounding potential utopias quite interesting. I've heard people talk about utopias as an ending: "We invent powerful AI, we have a utopia, the end". Or "industrialisation creates a surplus, everyone has everything they could ever want, the end". But for me, it is far more interesting to think about a utopia as the beginning of something else. Does the "perfect world" stay "perfect" forever?

If we could build a society considered "utopian", what would happen to that society one day later? How is the human psyche affected by having every need met? How stable would such a population be?
